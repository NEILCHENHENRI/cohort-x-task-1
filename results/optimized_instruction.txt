## Field Extraction Rules

### conditions
Extract the SPECIFIC disease(s) or medical condition(s) that the study investigates. Use the disease names as they appear in the article title and keywords — these are the most reliable sources. Do NOT output a symptom, imaging finding, broad category, or technology/method name. For example:
- If the title says "subarachnoid hemorrhage" and keywords include "vasospasm" and "delayed cerebral ischemia", output those specific conditions.
- If the title says "acute ischemic stroke", output "Acute Ischemic Stroke", not "unknown time of symptom onset".
- If the study is about "SARS-CoV-2 Infection" or "COVID-19", use the most specific clinical label (e.g., "SARS-CoV-2 Infection").
- Do NOT output technology names (e.g., "nanoradiopharmaceuticals") as conditions — identify the actual disease being studied.
- If the article is a review or general overview without a specific clinical population, look to the keywords and abstract for the primary disease focus.

### study_type
Classify as either INTERVENTIONAL or OBSERVATIONAL:
- INTERVENTIONAL: A treatment, drug, device, or procedure is actively assigned to participants (e.g., randomized controlled trials, clinical trials with an intervention arm).
- OBSERVATIONAL: No intervention is assigned; the study observes, monitors, or retrospectively analyzes participants (e.g., cohort studies, case-control studies, retrospective chart reviews, registry studies, review articles, biomarker studies without treatment assignment).
- Key signal: words like "randomized", "assigned to treatment", "placebo-controlled" → INTERVENTIONAL. Words like "observational", "retrospective", "registry", "cohort", "monitoring", "review", "biomarker study" → OBSERVATIONAL.
- IMPORTANT: Review articles and studies that only observe/analyze patients without assigning any treatment are OBSERVATIONAL, not INTERVENTIONAL.

### sex
Output one of: MALE, FEMALE, or ALL.
- Output ALL unless the study explicitly restricts enrollment to only one sex.
- Do not guess based on disease prevalence (e.g., prostate cancer studies should be MALE only if explicitly stated).
- When the article explicitly states enrollment is limited to one sex (e.g., "male patients", "women only"), output that sex.

### minimum_age
Output the minimum age eligibility criterion exactly as stated in the article (e.g., "18 Years", "65 Years").
- If the article does NOT explicitly state a minimum age using a specific number, output: Not Specified
- Do NOT infer or guess an age from context (e.g., "adults" does NOT mean "18 Years" unless the number 18 is explicitly written).
- IMPORTANT: Even if a minimum age seems implied, only report it if the exact number appears in the article text.

### maximum_age
Output the maximum age eligibility criterion exactly as stated in the article (e.g., "75 Years").
- If the article does NOT explicitly state a maximum age, output: Not Specified
- Do NOT infer or guess an age. Most articles do not state a maximum age — default to Not Specified unless explicitly mentioned.

### eligibility_criteria
Extract BOTH inclusion AND exclusion criteria as described in the article. Structure your output clearly:

Inclusion Criteria:
- [criterion 1]
- [criterion 2]
...

Exclusion Criteria:
- [criterion 1]
- [criterion 2]

Important notes:
- Be COMPREHENSIVE and SPECIFIC. Include every criterion mentioned: specific diagnoses, lab value thresholds, prior treatment requirements, performance status, age limits, imaging findings, etc.
- Do NOT summarize or paraphrase — extract the actual criteria as stated.
- Actively search for exclusion criteria in methods, study design, and patient selection sections — they are often present but easy to overlook.
- If no explicit exclusion criteria are mentioned, write "Exclusion Criteria: Not explicitly stated."
- If no explicit inclusion criteria are mentioned, write "Inclusion Criteria: Not explicitly stated."
- Do not include regulatory/ethical statements (e.g., IRB approval, informed consent language) as eligibility criteria.
- Do not include outcome definitions or statistical criteria as eligibility criteria.
- For review articles or articles with no human subject enrollment, note that eligibility criteria are not applicable or not stated.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Do NOT output a maximum_age or minimum_age unless the specific number is explicitly written in the article text.
2. Do NOT classify observational/retrospective studies or review articles as INTERVENTIONAL — only classify as INTERVENTIONAL if participants are actively assigned a treatment, drug, or procedure.
3. Do NOT name symptoms, imaging findings, mechanisms, or technologies as the condition — use the specific disease name.
4. Always look for and include exclusion criteria in eligibility_criteria — search throughout the methods and patient selection sections.
5. Do not fabricate eligibility criteria from general clinical practice — only extract what is explicitly stated in the article.
6. For sex: only restrict to MALE or FEMALE if the article explicitly states that enrollment is limited to that sex; otherwise output ALL.
7. Be thorough in eligibility_criteria — a short response likely means you missed criteria. Include specific thresholds, lab values, imaging criteria, and all subcriteria.
